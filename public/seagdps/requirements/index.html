Rate Requirements

Base Requirements

  • If your level has a very good gameplay, but the deco isn't the best, it can still be rated higher. For example: a level with Feature worthy deco, but it's rated Epic because it has great gameplay.
  • Longer levels get higher ratings, for example: "Our Time" by boxie.
  • Creativity will give higher ratings. For example: "Last Life" by ProfWhizz. The background is pretty empty and the structuring is pretty odd, but it's very creative and well-made.
  • Levels have to be at least 15 seconds.
  • Levels must be verified.
  • Levels should be consistent. Don't have one part be a 6 star but the next is an Extreme Demon. The same for deco: one part shouldn't be Feature and another is Mythic.
  • If a level's gameplay is considered very bad by a large majority of players, the level won't be rated even if the deco is rate worthy.

Each Rate Specific

  • Star Rate - The level needs to have some structuring. You can use simple 1.0 blocks or just solid blocks, the gameplay should be okay at least.
  • Feature - At least 30 seconds long. Your level needs to have pretty decent deco and good gameplay, otherwise great gameplay with relatively simple deco. For example: "Teresa and Maria" by CrazyFlyKite.
  • Epic - At least 30 seconds long. Your level should have good deco with great gameplay. For example: "adrenaline" by SeaCarrrot.
  • Legendary - At least 30 seconds long. Your level should have very good gameplay with excellent deco, and just overall a great level.
  • Mythic - Stupid joke level :3
